# Korean Professors @ Stanford University

## Gi-Wook Shin

- The most prominent Korean professor at Stanford!
- Professor of Sociology / Founding Director of the Korean Studies Program / Senior Fellow, Freeman Spogli Institute for International Studies / Professor, by courtesy, of East Asian Languages & Cultures
- gwshin@stanford.edu
- Specialty: historical-comparative and political sociologist whose research concentrates on social movements, nationalism, development, democracy, migration, and international relations
- Served as director of the Walter H. Shorenstein Asia-Pacific Research Center (APARC) from 2005 to 2025 —
- [신기욱 美스탠퍼드대 아·태 연구소장, 20년 만에 퇴임](https://www.chosun.com/international/international_general/2025/06/02/3DHIQDMDLBGNHJPKMBHQYCND5I/){:target="_blank"}

## Paul Kim

- Chief Technology Officer and Associate Dean of the Graduate School of Education
- phkim@stanford.edu
- Specialty - design of learning technologies, educational research, and community development

## Seung K. Kim

- Professor, Developmental Biology, Medicine - Endocrinology, Gerontology, & Metabolism
- seungkim@stanford.edu
- Specialty: pancreatic islet development and biology, diabetes

## Jin Hyung Lee

- Associate Professor @ Neurology and Neurological Sciences / Bioengineering / Neurosurgery
- ljinhy@stanford.edu
- Specialty: optogenetic fMRI and computational modeling of brain circuits, drawing on medical imaging, signal processing, genetics, and molecular biology

## Hyun Suk Park

- Assistant Professor, East Asian Languages and Cultures
- hyunpark@stanford.edu
- Specialty - Korean literature and culture from the 17th–19th century, working in both Korean and literary Sinitic (hanmun 漢文)

## Hyongsok Tom Soh

- W. M. Keck Foundation Professor of Electrical Engineering, Radiology (Diagnostic Sciences Laboratory) and of Bioengineering
- tsoh@stanford.edu
- Specialty: analytical biotechnology — high-throughput screening, directed evolution, and integrated biosensors.

# Korean Professors @ UC Berkeley

## Sewon Min

- Assistant Professor, Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences (EECS) / affiliated with Berkeley AI Research (BAIR) / Research Scientist, Allen Institute for AI (Ai2)
- sewonm@berkeley.edu
- Specialty: natural language processing and machine learning, with a focus on large language models — how they learn from and use very large text corpora
- Joined Berkeley EECS in July 2025; SNU CS undergrad (ranked 1st in department), PhD at the University of Washington

## Sung-Jin Oh

- Associate Professor, Department of Mathematics (also Adjunct Professor, KIAS)
- sjoh@math.berkeley.edu
- Specialty: geometric PDEs originating from physics — Yang-Mills equations, Einstein equations, Chern-Simons theories, wave maps, incompressible Euler/MHD equations
- 2026 Samsung Ho-Am Prize in Science (Physics & Mathematics); ICM 2026 invited speaker; KAIST undergrad, PhD Princeton (advisor: Sergiu Klainerman)

## Sug Woo Shin

- Professor, Department of Mathematics (also Distinguished Professor, KIAS)
- sug.woo.shin@berkeley.edu
- Specialty: number theory — automorphic forms, Shimura varieties, and the Langlands program
- Ho-Am Prize (2025); Sloan Fellowship (2013); two-time IMO gold medalist for Korea (perfect score in 1995); SNU undergrad, PhD Harvard (advisor: Richard Taylor)

## Jinsoo An

- Associate Professor + The Korea Foundation Chancellor's Chair in Korean Language & Culture, Department of East Asian Languages and Cultures (Korean Program)
- anjinsoo@berkeley.edu
- Specialty: Korean film history, East Asian cinema, film genre and authorship, history and memory, film censorship
- Tied to the Center for Korean Studies; author of *Parameters of Disavowal: Colonial Representation in South Korean Cinema* (UC Press, 2018); PhD in Film & TV from UCLA; joined Berkeley in 2012

## Steven S. Lee

- Associate Professor of English / C.K. Cho Chair, Center for Korean Studies
- stevenlee@berkeley.edu
- Specialty: 20th-century American literature, comparative ethnic studies, diaspora, Korean studies, Soviet/post-Soviet studies
- Author of *The Ethnic Avant-Garde: Minority Cultures and World Revolution* (Columbia UP, 2015); currently chairs Berkeley's Center for Korean Studies

## Sarah Song

- Professor of Law and Political Science (affiliated faculty in Philosophy), Jurisprudence and Social Policy program
- ssong@law.berkeley.edu
- Specialty: political and legal philosophy, citizenship and immigration law and policy, multiculturalism, freedom of speech and religion
- Born in Seoul, immigrated to the U.S. at age six; PhD in Political Science from Yale; as of early 2026, Associate Dean leading the JSP and Legal Studies programs

## John Yoo

- Emanuel S. Heller Professor of Law (at Berkeley Law since 1993); supervises the Korea Law Center, Public Law and Policy Program, and California Constitution Center
- yooj@law.berkeley.edu
- Specialty: constitutional law, administrative law, national security, international law, separation of powers
